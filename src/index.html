The Single Most Important Mindset Shift

This is NOT a transformation. It is NOT a finished product. It's a technical exploration. Discovering that something doesn't work is just as valuable as making it work — as long as we document the blocker clearly. The client loved this framing so much it was written explicitly: "Trying to AI-ify a process and it doesn't work — that is a success for this project."

Client & Context

Who, What & Why

The full picture before touching any tooling.

🏨

Who is Accor?

Global luxury hospitality group — Sofitel, Novotel, Ibis and 40+ other brands. Their CD&T (Corporate Digital & Technology) / Software Factory division is driving a large-scale AI transformation of software delivery across all product teams.

🔄

What is happening?

Accor is embedding AI across every PDLC (Product Development Lifecycle) stage for multiple delivery teams. First-wave team onboarding is targeted for September 2026. Before that, they need evidence of what works, what breaks, and what needs more investment.

⚡

Why this engagement exists?

Accor fears discovering blockers while live teams are already in flight — too late to fix. This is the pre-stage: prove everything on a safe sandbox, document every blocker, hand it over before transformation starts.

Our company — the delivery partner. We deliver from Bangkok, Thailand, working on-site at Accor's office daily (S Mitro Tower, near BTS). Team: 1 Engagement Manager + 1 AI Lead + 1 AI Product Owner + 4 AI Engineers.

What is a Work Unit?

A work unit is a discrete, time-boxed exploration that embeds AI into one specific capability of the PDLC. Each unit has a fixed scope, a clear "done" definition, and is validated entirely against the mock project — never on live Accor systems.

S — Special
S1 Initiation & S2 Wrap-Up — project bookends, not delivered work
T — Technical (T1–T12)
Each ~2 weeks. Delivered by AI engineers across two parallel technical streams
F — Functional (F1–F3)
Each ~3 weeks. Delivered by the functional stream — your stream

The order of units is decided during S1 (initiation) based on Accor priorities, and can be reprioritised at steering committees. Units marked ⭐ are in scope for this engagement.

Risks & Assumptions

What Could Go Wrong — And What Doesn't Apply

This is a de-risking engagement by nature. A well-documented blocker is as valuable as a working configuration.

✅
Risk Acceptance Statement

Some work units may conclude that AI capabilities don't yet work well enough, or require more investment than anticipated within Accor's environment. These are valid success outcomes. A unit that identifies and clearly documents a blocker is as successful as a unit that produces a working configuration.

Key Risks

Key Assumptions

  • PALO IT delivers from Thailand; on-site at Accor office daily
  • Accor provides forked, sanitized mock project before T0
  • All tool access (GitLab, Jira, AWS, ServiceNow, Splunk…) granted in first week (S1)
  • Named Accor counterparts are committed for the full engagement duration
  • AI tools are already selected — AccorGPT (LiteLLM + Bedrock) + Cline + Claude Code
  • AccorGPT is operational and accessible from Day 1
  • AI model capabilities are current-generation (Claude Sonnet/Opus, GPT-4+) — no fine-tuning required
  • Mock project business logic stays simple; complexity is in PDLC flow, not the app
  • Accor provides existing standards/conventions documentation for each PDLC stage
  • No production deployment or production data access required

What is Explicitly Excluded

  • Production deployment or production data access
  • Tool selection or vendor evaluation
  • Custom AI model training or fine-tuning
  • Organizational transformation or team restructuring
  • Full-scale rollout to Accor delivery teams (post-engagement)
  • Integration with every tool in Accor landscape
  • Performance/load testing, penetration testing, compliance certification
  • Third-party tool procurement or licensing management
  • Delivery of any unit without active Accor counterpart involvement
  • Guaranteed productivity gains — this is an exploratory engagement
